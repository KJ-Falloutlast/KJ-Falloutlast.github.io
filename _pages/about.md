# Kun Hu | 胡坤

I am a third-year master student in Mechanical Engineering at the CoalMine Robot Lab, China University of Mining and Technology, advised by Prof. Menggang Li.

My research interests center on robust localization and mapping for coal mine robots, especially LiDAR-inertial odometry, UWB-aided SLAM, wheel odometry fusion, and multi-sensor localization in GPS-denied, feature-degraded, and harsh underground environments. I am particularly interested in building reliable robotic perception and localization systems for extreme industrial scenarios where LiDAR degeneration, UWB outage, wheel slip, and long-term structural changes make conventional SLAM systems fragile.

I have published papers in robotics and engineering venues, including IROS, Journal of Field Robotics, IEEE Internet of Things Journal, IEEE Transactions on Instrumentation and Measurement. My recent work focuses on degradation-resistant localization, adaptive multi-sensor fusion, and reliable SLAM systems for long, narrow, and structurally repetitive coal mine tunnels.

## Featured Research

My research focuses on robust spatial intelligence for underground robots, specifically including LiDAR-inertial odometry, LiDAR-Inertial-UWB-Wheel fusion, degradation-aware SLAM, adaptive localization, and multi-sensor fusion for GPS-denied coal mine tunnels.

### DURAL: Degradation-resistant Robust Adaptive Localization by LiDAR-Inertial-UWB-Wheel Fusion for Coal Mine Robots

![DURAL](/images/dural.jpg){: .align-left style="width: 260px; margin-right: 20px;"}

**Kun Hu**, Menggang Li, et al.
*Journal of Field Robotics*, 2025
[[Paper]](#) / [[Code]](#)

DURAL is a degradation-resistant adaptive localization framework for coal mine robots. It fuses LiDAR, IMU, UWB, and wheel odometry to achieve robust localization in GPS-denied, feature-degraded, and harsh underground tunnel environments.

### CM-LIUW-Odometry: Robust and High-Precision LiDAR-Inertial-UWB-Wheel Odometry for Extreme Degradation Coal Mine Tunnels

![CM-LIUW-Odometry](/images/cm-liuw.jpg){: .align-left style="width: 260px; margin-right: 20px;"}

**Kun Hu**, Menggang Li, Zhiwen Jin, Chaoquan Tang, Eryi Hu, Gongbo Zhou
*IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS)*, 2025
[[arXiv]](#) / [[Code]](https://github.com/KJ-Falloutlast/CM-LIUW-Odometry)

CM-LIUW-Odometry is a multimodal SLAM framework based on LiDAR-inertial-UWB-wheel fusion. It improves global consistency with UWB constraints and maintains robust localization during UWB-outage periods using wheel odometry, nonholonomic constraints, lever-arm compensation, and adaptive motion-mode switching.

### Adaptive-LIO: Enhancing Robustness and Precision through Environmental Adaptation in LiDAR Inertial Odometry

![Adaptive-LIO](/images/adaptive-lio.jpg){: .align-left style="width: 260px; margin-right: 20px;"}

Adaptive-LIO enhances LiDAR-inertial odometry through environmental adaptation, including adaptive segmentation, IMU saturation and fault detection, and multi-resolution voxel maps.
